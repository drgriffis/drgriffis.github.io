Research interests

My two main areas of research are:

  1. Applying methods from the Natural Language Processing (NLP) field to problems in the clinical health informatics domain, particularly working with clinical text (EHR).
  2. Developing computational methods for generalizable semantic representations.

Other interests include theoretical machine learning, information retrieval, and the ethics of AI.

I also translate stories in Russian! One of them (translated as joint work with other students at Carleton College) was published in 2015.

Brief Bio

I am a PhD student (2014-???) at Ohio State, in the department of Computer Science and Engineering (CSE). I'm co-advised by Dr. Eric Fosler-Lussier (CSE) and Dr. Albert Lai (Department of Biomedical Informatics).

I'm currently working as a Pre-Doctoral Fellow with the Epidemiology and Biostatistics section of the National Institutes of Health Clinical Center, looking at how we can use NLP techniques to improve the workflow of the Social Security Administration's disability determination process.
